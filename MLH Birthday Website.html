  • Starting in 2013

    Beginnings

    Major League Hacking was co-founded by Jonathan Gottried and Mike Swift in 2013. Since its inception, the organization has ran over 200 weekend long complitions worlwide each year.

  • Feburary 2014

    Hackcon

    The first Hackcon was held at Alley NYC in 2014 as a 2 day conference for hackercommunity leaders and organizers to learn from each other and work to improve Hackathons.

  • June 2014

    Hackathon Hackers Facebook

    The Hackathon Hackers Facebook page was establishd as a way for bring the hackathon communuty as a whole closer together. This page became a resources for many organizers as it included information from past Hackcons as well.

  • October 22, 2015

    MLH Community Council

    The MLH Community Council is boared of active comunity memebers who represesnt various roles within the organization itself such as hackers, organizers, sponsers, etc. The purpose of which is to help develop ways to tailor to student hackers and bring a variety of perspectives to the table when dicussing important comunity issues.

  • During 2016

    Certified B Corporation

    Since MLH is powered by one community of 65,000 hackers in 16 different countries, Major League Hacking became a Certified B Corporation in 2016. This means that they had to take into account the impacts of the community from their decisions, and not just their investors and sponsors.

  • September 29th 2019

    MLH x APAC

    In order to assure that as many hackers around the word have the oppertunity compete in these competitions, MLA has officially launched in the Asia-Pacific for hackers in India, australia, Singapore, etc.

  • May 3rd 2022

    TechTogether Acquisition

    In order to promote and create an inclusive envoroment for all progammers, MLH made the descision to aquire TechTogether, a organization whos' events specifically serve hackers who pertain to marginalized groups.
